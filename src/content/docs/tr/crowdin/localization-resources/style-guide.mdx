With a style guide, you can create, store, and manage all your project's formatting and tone rules in one place. The main aim of a style guide is to ensure consistency in voice, style, and terminology usage across translations, helping translators understand exactly how your brand should sound in different languages.

Stil kılavuzları, [Çeviri Belleği (ÇB)](/translation-memory/) ve [Sözlük](/glossary/) ile aynı seviyede yerel bir kaynak olarak işlev görür. They provide human translators with a clear reference during the translation process and serve as foundational instructions for Crowdin AI features like AI Auto-Translation, AI Assistant, and AI QA Checks.

## Creating Style Guides

You can create style guides and populate them with content by generating them with AI, writing them from scratch, or uploading existing files in MD, PDF, DOCX, or XLSX formats. These style guides can then be assigned to the relevant projects and specific target languages as needed.

To create a style guide, follow these steps:

<Steps>
  1. Profilinizin ana sayfasını açın ve sol kenar çubuğundaki **Stil Kılavuzları**’nı seçin.
  2. **Stil Kılavuzu Oluştur**’a tıklayın. <Image src={createStyleGuide} alt="Create Style Guide" />
  3. In the left panel of the appeared dialog, configure the following settings:
     * **Name** &ndash; Name your style guide.
     * **Projects** &ndash; Select **All** or **Selected** to assign the style guide to specific projects.
     * **Language** &ndash; *(Optional)* Restrict the style guide to specific target languages. If left empty, it applies to all languages in the assigned projects.
  4. Move to the right side of the dialog and choose your preferred method for creating the content:
     * **Generate with AI** &ndash; Describe your brand voice, target audience, and specific constraints in the **Generate from Description** field. Crowdin’in yerleşik yapay zekasının sizin için kapsamlı bir Markdown dosyası oluşturmasına izin vermek için **Taslak Oluştur**’a tıklayın. You can then review and edit it if needed in the Markdown editor.
     * **Crowdin’de Yaz** &ndash; Stil kılavuzunuzu sıfırdan yazmak için yerleşik Markdown düzenleyicisini kullanın.
     * **Upload file** &ndash; Upload an existing `.md`, `.pdf`, `.docx`, or `.xlsx` file. If you upload a Markdown file, the system will automatically populate the built-in editor with its content.
  5. **Oluştur**’a tıklayın. <Image src={createStyleGuideDialog} alt="Create style guide dialog" class="width-lg" />
</Steps>

## Optimizing Style Guides for AI

To use your style guide with AI-powered features (like auto-translation or the AI Assistant), the AI requires a concise summary of your rules to include in its prompts. This is managed in the **AI-Ready Version** section of your style guide.

Eğer stil kılavuzunuzu **Açıklamadan Oluştur** yöntemini kullanarak oluşturursanız, Crowdin sizin için otomatik olarak YZ-Hazır Sürümü oluşturur ve durumu **Hazır** olur.

If you upload a file or write the guide from scratch, you can either write this summary manually or click **Generate** to have the AI summarize the full guide for you.

The system tracks whether your AI instructions are up-to-date with your current style guide content. You can view this status in the **AI Version** column on the **Style Guides** page:

* **None** &ndash; The style guide does not have an AI-Ready Version and will not be passed to the AI in prompts.
* **Ready** &ndash; The AI-Ready Version is fully up-to-date with the current style guide content.
* **Outdated** &ndash; The style guide content was modified, but the AI-Ready Version was not regenerated.
  <Aside type="caution">
      If a style guide is **Outdated**, it will still be passed to the AI during translation or QA tasks, but the AI will use the old instructions, which may lead to inaccuracies. Always review and update the AI-Ready Version after editing your style guide.
  </Aside>

## Managing Style Guides

Stil kılavuzlarınızı doğrudan profil ana sayfanızdaki **Stil Kılavuzları** sayfasından yönetebilirsiniz. This includes searching, filtering, editing, downloading, and deleting them.

### Searching, Filtering, and Sorting Style Guides

By default, all style guides are displayed on the **Style Guides** page. The table displays the style guide's *Name*, *AI Version*, assigned *Projects*, *Languages*, *Owner*, and *Created* date.

To find a specific style guide, use the **Search** field. Stil kılavuzları listesini süzmek için **Süzgeçler**’e <Icon name="mdi:filter-variant" class="inline-icon" /> tıklayın ve mevcut seçenekleri kullanın:

* **Projects** &ndash; Filter by assigned projects (**All** or a specific project).
* **Languages** &ndash; Filter by assigned languages (**All** or a specific language).
* **Owner** &ndash; Filter by the user who created the style guide.
* **Created** &ndash; Filter by creation date (**All** or **Custom Range**).

To sort style guides, click the **Name** column header once to sort in ascending order and click again to sort in descending order.

### Editing Style Guides

If you need to adjust your existing style guide, you can simply edit it.

To edit a style guide, follow these steps:

<Steps>
  1. Profilinizin ana sayfasını açın ve sol kenar çubuğundaki **Stil Kılavuzları**’nı seçin.
  2. Gerekli stil kılavuzunun yanındaki <Icon name="mdi:dots-horizontal" class="inline-icon" /> simgesine tıklayın ve **Düzenle**’yi seçin.
  3. Modify the style guide as needed and click **Save** to save changes.
</Steps>

When editing a style guide, you'll find the **Style guide metadata** section on the left panel. This information provides a clear audit trail for collaboration and troubleshooting. By tracking creation and modification data, you can understand who created or last updated the rules and when. The section contains the following read-only details:

* **Created by** – The user who created the style guide.
* **Created** – The date and time the style guide was created.
* **Modified by** – The user who last modified the style guide.
* **Last modified** – The date and time the style guide was last modified.

Editing style guides allows you to update and improve them as needed to ensure they remain effective and aligned with your evolving project requirements.

### Downloading Style Guides

You can download your style guides for offline reference and reuse them elsewhere.

To download a style guide, follow these steps:

<Steps>
  1. Profilinizin ana sayfasını açın ve sol kenar çubuğundaki **Stil Kılavuzları**’nı seçin.
  2. Gerekli sözlükte <Icon name="mdi:dots-horizontal" class="inline-icon" /> simgesine tıklayın ve **Düzenle**’yi seçin.  Alternatively, just double-click on the needed style guide.
  3. Click <Icon name="mdi:download" class="inline-icon" /> **Download** in the editor toolbar.
</Steps>

<Aside type="tip">
  Project members can also download style guides directly from the Editor while working on a project.
</Aside>

### Deleting Style Guides

To delete style guides, follow these steps:

<Steps>
  1. Profilinizin ana sayfasını açın ve sol kenar çubuğundaki **Stil Kılavuzları**’nı seçin.
  2. Delete the style guides using one of the following methods:
     * **Tekli stil kılavuzu**: Stil kılavuzunun yanındaki <Icon name="mdi:dots-horizontal" class="inline-icon" /> simgesine tıklayın ve **Sil**’i seçin.
     * **Multiple style guides**: Select the checkboxes next to the style guides you want to delete, or top checkbox to delete all, then click <Icon name="mdi:delete" class="inline-icon" /> at the top of the list.
  3. Onay ileti öğesinde silme işlemini onaylayın.
</Steps>

<Aside type="caution">
  Bir stil kılavuzunu silmek, stil kılavuzunu ve içerdiği tüm kuralları Crowdin hesabınızdan kalıcı olarak kaldıracaktır. This action cannot be undone.
</Aside>

## Style Guides in AI QA Checks

When the AI-powered QA check is enabled in your project, it automatically incorporates all assigned style guides into its evaluation prompt. The AI verifies that each submitted translation aligns with the tone, voice, and stylistic guidelines defined in your style guides.

You don't need to manually select a style guide in your AI prompt settings. The system automatically evaluates translations against all the style guides assigned to the project.
